Title: Joint Pain During Exercise
Category: musculoskeletal
Symptoms: pain in joints, swelling, stiffness, reduced range of motion, clicking or popping
Causes: poor form, sudden increase in activity, inadequate warm-up, underlying joint issues, improper footwear
Guidance: Joint pain during exercise should not be ignored. Mild discomfort that resolves may be normal, but sharp pain or persistent issues indicate need for form correction or rest. Do not push through sharp joint pain.
Prevention: Use proper exercise form. Warm up 5-10 minutes. Increase intensity gradually. Wear appropriate footwear. Include strength training for stability.
When to see doctor: Sharp joint pain, significant swelling, joint that feels unstable, pain that worsens or persists for weeks
