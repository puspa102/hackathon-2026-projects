Title: Dehydration After Exercise
Category: fitness
Symptoms: dizziness, lightheadedness, fatigue, dry mouth, reduced urination
Causes: inadequate water intake, excessive sweating, hot weather, high intensity exercise
Guidance: Dehydration after exercise is common when fluid intake is insufficient. Drink water gradually (not all at once) with electrolytes if exercising longer than 60 minutes. Typical signs include thirst, dark urine, dizziness, and fatigue. Rehydrate within 30 minutes of exercise completion.
Prevention: Drink 16-20 oz of water 2-3 hours before exercise, 8-10 oz 20 minutes before, and 7-10 oz every 10-20 minutes during exercise. Drink 16-24 oz after exercise for every pound lost.
When to see doctor: Severe persistent dizziness, confusion, rapid heartbeat, or lack of sweating despite heat exposure
