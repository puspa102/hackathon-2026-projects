Title: Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness (DOMS)
Category: fitness
Symptoms: muscle soreness 24-72 hours after exercise, stiffness, reduced range of motion, mild swelling
Causes: new exercise routine, increased intensity or volume, eccentric exercises (lengthening under load), lack of warm-up
Guidance: DOMS is normal muscular adaptation and typically peaks 24-72 hours after exercise. Soreness does not indicate injury unless accompanied by sharp pain or significant swelling. Soreness improves as body adapts.
Prevention: Gradually increase exercise intensity. Warm up properly. Cool down and stretch. Stay hydrated. Adequate protein aids recovery.
When to see doctor: Severe swelling, inability to move affected muscle, sharp stabbing pain, or soreness lasting beyond 5-7 days
