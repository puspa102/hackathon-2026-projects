Title: Elevated Resting Heart Rate
Category: cardiovascular
Symptoms: resting heart rate consistently above 100 bpm, rapid pulse at rest, sometimes with palpitations
Causes: stress, caffeine, dehydration, fever, hyperthyroidism, overtraining, cardiovascular issues
Guidance: Normal resting heart rate is 60-100 bpm. Trained athletes may have lower rates. Consistently elevated resting heart rate may indicate overtraining, stress, or other issues. Check resting heart rate in morning before getting up.
Prevention: Manage stress. Reduce caffeine. Ensure adequate recovery days. Stay hydrated. Get 7-9 hours sleep.
When to see doctor: Resting heart rate consistently above 100 bpm, accompanied by dizziness or chest pain, or sudden significant increase in resting heart rate
